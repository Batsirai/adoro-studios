# AlreadyLoved Copy Rewrite — Kieran's Storytelling Framework
> *Applied to alreadylovedkids.com homepage using Kieran Drew's 6-step sales storytelling framework*
> *Source: https://x.com/ItsKieranDrew/status/2035382272556498973*

---

## Kieran's 6-Step Framework

1. **Begin in the action** — Emotion + excitement, open the loop
2. **The setup** — Show why alternatives suck
3. **The emotional cost** — Let them make their own conclusions
4. **Paint a picture of your product** — Pitch with confidence, stimulate desire
5. **Remove uncertainty** — Remove risk, guarantee, no-brainer
6. **Clear, urgent CTA** — Easy, frictionless, describe the life change

---

## SECTION-BY-SECTION ANALYSIS

### HERO SECTION
**Current:**
> "Tell them who they are before the world does."
> "Someone will shape your child's inner voice. Let it be you."

**Strength:** Clear positioning, strong principle.
**Weakness:** Statement, not scene. Reader observes rather than feels. No gut-punch moment.

**Rewrite — Begin in the action:**
> "She asked me, 'Mommy, am I God's favorite?' And I realized — I didn't have the words."
>
> "Someone will give my daughter her first answer about who she is. I want it to be me."
>
> [Start Tonight. Free.]

**Why:** Opens with a real moment every parent recognizes. Lets them feel it before they understand why.

---

### THE ALTERNATIVES SUCK (Softened)
**Current:** "Not just another personalized book" — weak, apologetic framing.
**Problem:** Doesn't create enough friction with alternatives.

**Rewrite:**
> "Most books teach your child about animals, colors, and adventure."
>
> "None of them whisper the truth your 4-year-old needs to hear: 'You are loved exactly as you are. Right now. By God himself.'"
>
> "That's the voice that shapes everything — who she dates at 16, what she says no to at 22, who she becomes at 33."
>
> "We wrote that book."

**Why:** Positions alternatives as entertaining but identity-blind. Doesn't trash them — just shows the gap clearly.

---

### EMOTIONAL COST (Already Strong — Keep This)
**Current:**
> "There's a voice your child will soon hear. It whispers and roars: 'You're not enough. You don't belong.'"
> "Right now, you have a sacred window where your voice matters most."

**Verdict: KEEP THIS. It's the best section on the page.** It does exactly what Kieran says — show the cost through emotion, let the reader conclude on their own.

---

### PAINT A PICTURE OF THE PRODUCT
**Current:** Process steps are clear but clinical.
**Problem:** "Tell us about your child" and "Preview & Purchase" read like a form, not a transformation.

**Rewrite — Paint the scene at step 3:**
> "It's bedtime. She's had a hard day — you can see it in her shoulders."
>
> "You open AlreadyLoved and her name is on every page. Her face is the hero. The last page says: 'You are loved. You are wanted. You are enough.'"
>
> "She whispers it back. And that's the voice she takes to school tomorrow."
>
> [See it in 3 minutes. Free.]

**Why:** Replace clinical "process" language with the actual bedtime moment. Make the product real before they buy.

---

### REMOVE UNCERTAINTY (Already Strong — Keep + Amplify)
**Current:**
> "Free 4-page preview. No credit card required. Approve every page before printing."
> "Preview free. Approve every page before it prints. Love it or don't ship it."

**Verdict: KEEP. Add one more layer:**
> "Not sure? Start free. See your child on every page. Only approve if it makes you smile."
>
> "Love it or we don't print it. That's our promise."

---

### CLEAR, URGENT CTA
**Current:** "Create Your Book. Free preview. Read it tonight."
**Problem:** "Create Your Book" is vague. What does creating entail?

**Rewrite:**
> "3 minutes. One photo. Tonight, she sees herself in a story she'll remember forever."
>
> [Start Free — See Your Child's Book Tonight]
>
> Free preview. No credit card. Approve every page.

## FULL REWRITE — HERO SECTION (Drop-in Ready)

**Headline:** "She asked me, 'Am I God's favorite?' And I realized — I didn't have the words."

**Subhead:** "Someone will answer that question for your child. Let it be you."

**CTA:** [Start Tonight. Free.]

**Below headline — 3 trust lines:**
- Free 4-page preview — no credit card required
- See your child on every page before you buy
- Approve every page. Love it or we don't print it.

## WHAT'S ALREADY WORKING (Don't Touch)

1. **The voice section** — "There's a voice your child will soon hear..." — perfectly executed emotional cost. Keep verbatim.
2. **"Love it or don't ship it"** — best risk-reversal line on the page.
3. **The timeline** ("At 5 she walks in... At 16 she doesn't feel pressured...") — paints a future worth fighting for.
4. **"Say What Lasts. Tonight."** — excellent footer tagline.

---

## PRIORITY ORDER FOR CHANGES

1. **HERO** (highest impact — rewrite first)
2. **"Not just another book" section** (replace with alternatives gap framing)
3. **Step 3 description** (add the bedtime scene painting)
4. **CTA language** (make more specific and visual)
5. Test the old vs new with an A/B test if traffic allows

## NOTES FOR AI AGENTS (Zo, Droid, etc.)

When writing for AlreadyLoved:
- Never open with features. Open with a MOMENT.
- Never explain what the product does. SHOW what it FEELS like.
- The alternative is not "not buying" — it's "the world telling your child who they are first"
- Risk reversal is a feature, not an apology. Say it with confidence.
- Keep the theological framing gentle. Trust the Holy Spirit, don't explain Him.
